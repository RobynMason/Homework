All About Cupcakes

The History of Cupcakes

The earliest description of a cupcake was in 1796, when a recipe was written in "American Cookery" by Amelia Simmons. The earliest description of the term cupcake itself was in 1828 in Eliza Leslie's cookbook. A standard cupcake uses the same basic ingredients as standard-sized cakes: butter, sugar, eggs, and flour. Nearly any recipe that is suitable for a layer cake can be used to bake cupcakes.

Easy Cupcake Recipe

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 cup softened butter
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
Preheat oven to 375F. Line muffin cups with papers. Cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in eggs one at a time. Combine flour, baking powder and salt. Add flour mixture alternating with milk.Beat until mixed well. Stir in vanilla. Divide evenly among pans and bake for 18 minutes.

Easy Frosting Recipe

  • 1 cup of unsalted butter (2 sticks)
  • 4.5 cups of powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract
  • 4-5 tablespoons of heavy cream or whole milk

Beat the softened butter using medium speed with an electric mixer until it is soft and creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar while mixing. Occasionally scrape the sides of the bowl to make sure all ingredients are mixed in. Once the butter and sugar are creamy add in heavy cream or milk and vanilla extract. Beat with mixer until it reaches the desired consistency.
